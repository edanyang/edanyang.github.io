Methodology

Brand-building Principles

Brand-building runs on different beliefs than marketing.
Four principles I keep returning to.

01

A brand is not content — a brand is a defined concept asset.

"Doing brand" is widely understood as "making content, producing assets, running campaigns." But a brand's actual core is an externally recognized, citable, retrievable concept asset. The former is traffic; the latter is inventory.

02

Cross-disciplinary construction is rarer than depth in a single field.

Most brand narratives circulate within the marketing-school loop of product–user–scenario. Bringing in resources from psychology, cognitive science, behavioral research, and work design — that's the scarce position. And scarcity is what gives pricing power.

03

A counter-consensus position matters more than incremental optimization.

Identifying "what NOT to do" under multi-constraint conditions is rarer than figuring out "what to do" — and doesn't depend on budget scale. Choosing not to do A when everyone else is doing A doesn't take cleverness. It takes judgment density.

04

In the AI era, brand assets are retrievable concept origins.

Over the next 3–5 years, the real distribution channel for brand content won't just be social media algorithms — it'll be generative AI systems. Schema.org DefinedTerm, hreflang, Article Schema — these technical details determine whether a brand's concepts enter the AI's "verified concept origin" layer.

Concept System · Decision Tax of Setup

A Working Vocabulary
Proposed by Edan Yang · 2026.02  ·  First Published at Mukiya · 2026.04

A named pain is easier to discuss, cite, and remember than an unnamed one.

CORE TERM
Decision Tax of Setup

The cognitive surcharge levied on every act of mobile work — paid in fragments, spent before any task begins.

Every act of mobile work begins with a hidden ledger entry — small decisions, position adjustments, environmental rebuilding — paid in fragments before the actual task starts.

SUB-TERM 01
Work Noise

Everything in modern work that is not the work itself — notifications, device switches, context jumps, friction between applications.

Modern work is increasingly composed of things that aren't the work — sounds, switches, jumps, friction. Not the content of work, but the energy of work.

SUB-TERM 02
Setup Fatigue

The hidden cost of rebuilding a complete work system (screen, power, device positioning, posture) each time the environment changes.

This fatigue accumulates 1.5–2× faster than task-driven fatigue, but rarely shows up in any cost ledger.

SUB-TERM 03
Cognitive Load Check

A method that converts the abstract cognitive load into individually measurable values across four tiers.

Based on (tools used today × deep problems tackled today), it outputs a 4-tier load classification with corresponding recovery recommendations. Interactive tool below.
